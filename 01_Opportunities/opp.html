  • Volunteering with UNICEF

    UNICEF works with volunteers primarily through the United Nations Volunteers (UNV) programme. If you have an undergraduate degree, several years of work experience and are interested in volunteer opportunities around the world, you may be eligible to become a UNV.

    The UNV programme recruits and places volunteers in assignments throughout the United Nations system, including at UNICEF. UNICEF does not recruit UNVs directly but through the UNV programme. To be considered, please visit the UNV candidate signup page and register your profile.

    Volunteer opportunities are also offered through our National Committees.

    Why Volunteer at UNICEF?

    •Meet like-minded professional volunteers from around the world
    •Help communities in need to improve their quality of life.
    •An opportunity to broaden your skills and enrich your career.
    •Be part of UNICEF’s work defending the rights of children and young people.

  • Food Pantry Van Loading and Unloading Volunteers

    Rockland Community Action Program is seeking volunteers who can assist with loading food into its van at a regional distribution site and unload it again upon arrival at its food pantry (about 5 miles—or 15 minutes—away). Volunteers will meet at the Cornell Cooperative Extension parking lot in Stony Point to help load the Haverstraw Community Action van with food, then follow the van (using their own cars) to the Haverstraw Community Action site in Haverstraw to help unload the food.

    This opportunity will take place once or twice a month for 1.5 hours; help out as much or as little as you'd like! Volunteers must be able to lift about 30 pounds. This is an outdoor opportunity, however volunteers are asked to wear masks at all times and practice safe social distancing wherever possible. Please wear comfortable clothing and closed-toe shoes; latex gloves will be provided.
